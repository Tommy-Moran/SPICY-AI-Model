# Data Sharing Statement

**Manuscript:** "Clinical Evaluation of SPICY-AI in a Rapid Access Chest Pain Clinic: A Comparative Validation Study of a Novel, Locally Deployed AI Model versus Physicians and GPT-4o"

**Corresponding author:** Dr Thomas Moran (thomas.moran@ths.tas.gov.au)

## What data will be shared

- De-identified Likert scores and error/hallucination indicators for all 150 plans used in the analysis (50 cases × 3 sources), deposited as `scores_long_v3.csv`.
- De-identified patient demographics for all 50 cases (age, sex, cardiovascular risk factors, absolute cardiovascular risk), deposited as `demographics.csv`.
- Summary statistics (mean, SD, median, IQR) per domain per group.
- All pairwise Mann-Whitney U and Fisher exact test results.
- All analysis code (see the Code Availability Statement).
- Publication-quality figures (300-DPI PNG, PDF, TIFF) and tabulated statistical outputs.

## What data will NOT be shared

- Raw, identifiable patient clinical summaries, physician-authored plans, and the full generated SPICY-AI / GPT-4o outputs, because these contain indirectly identifying clinical detail about Royal Hobart Hospital RACPC attendees and are covered by the approved HREC protocol (2025_ETH01927).
- The SPICY-AI knowledge-base corpus insofar as it incorporates institution-specific RHH RACPC operating protocols — these are the property of the Royal Hobart Hospital and will be shared only under a data-use agreement.

## How data will be shared

De-identified data and analysis artefacts will be deposited on Zenodo with a DOI at the time of publication and cross-linked from the GitHub repository. Requests for additional data (including the SPICY-AI knowledge base manifest and institutional protocol snapshots) can be directed to the corresponding author and will be considered on a case-by-case basis, subject to approval by the RHH HREC and the RHH Department of Cardiology.

## When data will be available

From the date of publication, indefinitely.

## With whom

Researchers who provide a methodologically sound proposal and whose requested use complies with the HREC-approved purpose.
